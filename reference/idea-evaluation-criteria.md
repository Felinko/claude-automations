# Startup Idea Evaluation Criteria

Used by the `startup-idea-finder` skill to score each niche. Score each dimension 1–5. Maximum total: **25 points**.

---

## Criteria

### 1. Problem Frequency (1–5)
How often does this problem appear across threads and subreddits?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Mentioned once or twice; may be an edge case |
| 2 | A few scattered mentions |
| 3 | Regularly appears in relevant communities |
| 4 | Frequently mentioned across multiple subreddits |
| 5 | Ubiquitous — shows up constantly, many people actively seeking solutions |

---

### 2. AI Leverage Potential (1–5)
Can AI provide a solution that is dramatically (10x) better than what exists today?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | AI adds marginal value; traditional software suffices |
| 2 | AI could help slightly but isn't essential |
| 3 | AI meaningfully improves the solution |
| 4 | AI enables a solution that wasn't previously feasible |
| 5 | AI is the core unlock — without it, this problem can't be solved well |

---

### 3. Market Size Signal (1–5)
How large and accessible is the potential customer base?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Tiny niche, few potential customers |
| 2 | Small but defined market |
| 3 | Moderate market with clear segments |
| 4 | Large market with many potential customers |
| 5 | Massive market — millions of potential users or large B2B spend |

---

### 4. Solution Gap (1–5)
How poor are the current solutions? Is the market undersupplied?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Strong existing solutions; market is well-served |
| 2 | Decent solutions exist but have known gaps |
| 3 | Mediocre solutions; significant room for improvement |
| 4 | Weak solutions; people are using workarounds |
| 5 | No real solution exists; people are doing this manually or going without |

---

### 5. Monetization Clarity (1–5)
How clear is the path to charging money?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Unclear who pays or how much |
| 2 | Some monetization path but weak signal |
| 3 | Clear customer type but fuzzy willingness to pay |
| 4 | Clear customer + reasonable pricing comp exists |
| 5 | Obvious B2B buyer, clear budget, or users explicitly say they'd pay |

---

## Score Interpretation

| Total Score | Interpretation |
|-------------|---------------|
| 22–25 | Strong signal — prioritize for deeper research |
| 17–21 | Promising — worth exploring further |
| 12–16 | Moderate — proceed with caution; validate assumptions |
| Below 12 | Weak signal — deprioritize |
